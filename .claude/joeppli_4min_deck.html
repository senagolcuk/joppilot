⏱ 38 sec · Weak link, Sammelstelle distance, ERZ 832 bags, no household data
"Circular economy has a weak link. The system rests on one motivated person in every household.

In suburbs and low-density cantons, average distance to a Sammelstelle is five hundred metres, and they're open just twice a week. That's enough to leave the elderly, the car-free, and the time-poor completely out of the loop.

Last week, Zürich hand-sorted 832 Züri-Säcke and found that forty percent of them should have been recycled.

And no one, not the citizens, not the municipality, actually knows how much is being recycled at the household, street, or neighbourhood level." ~70 words · 28 sec

⏱ 32 sec · OAD · Vertical AV (SBB) · BAFU mandates
"Why now? Three things lined up at the same time.

First, in 2025, the Swiss Ordinance on Automated Driving came into force, making driverless operation legal.

Second, the market chose its winner. Investors are backing vertical AV: city-scale services like shuttles and package delivery, not robotaxis. SBB itself has come out against robotaxis and only supports roboshuttles.

And third, BAFU's mandates are tightening. Reuse and recycling now take priority over incineration.

Regulation enables us. Regulation forces cities to need us." ~75 words · 28 sec

⏱ 32 sec · CHF 1.5B Swiss / CHF 30B EU, 50 vehicles by 2030 = CHF 6M ARR (0.4%)
"In Switzerland, one and a half billion francs is spent every year collecting six million tonnes of waste. Across the EU, it's a thirty-billion-franc market.

Our model is simple. One vehicle covers half a square kilometre of urban zone. By 2030, with fifty vehicles in operation, we reach six million francs in annual recurring revenue. That's zero-point-four percent of the Swiss market." ~40 words · 18 sec
